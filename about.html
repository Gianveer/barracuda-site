Electronic contract manufacturing — Somerset West

One factory, from bare board to boxed product.

Barracuda Holdings has built electronics under contract since 2010. The company is privately owned and owner-driven — its founding partners each bring more than 20 years in contract manufacturing. Work runs from surface-mount assembly through inspection, rework, finishing and test, under one roof in Cape Town.

The facility

180 people on a 2,800 m² floor in Somerset West.

Barracuda runs its lines from Bataleur Park in the Olive Grove Industrial Estate, Ou Paardevlei Road. Placement, soldering, inspection, rework and machining share the same building, so a board does not leave the site between steps.

Capabilities

The full process, grouped by stage.

Every line below is run in-house. Two advanced SMD lines place components at 258,500 per hour; from there a board can be soldered, inspected, reworked, coated, marked and machined without leaving the building.

01 Assembly

  • SMD assembly Advanced surface-mount lines placing 258,500 components per hour.
  • Wave soldering Through-hole soldering for mixed-technology boards.
  • Flexi & flexi-rigid PCB assembly Assembly on flexible and flexi-rigid circuit boards.
  • Potting & encapsulation Resin potting and encapsulation for protected assemblies.

02 Inspection & rework

  • X-ray inspection Checks joints hidden under packages, including BGA and LGA.
  • BGA / LGA rework Removal and replacement of ball-grid and land-grid array parts.
  • Testing & packaging Functional test and pack-out to the customer's specification.

03 Finishing & mechanical

  • Conformal coating Protective coating for boards exposed to moisture and dust.
  • Laser marking Permanent part and product marking.
  • CNC machining Machined mechanical parts and enclosures on site.

04 Engineering services

  • Software development Firmware and software written alongside the hardware build.
  • Stencil engineering Stencil design and preparation for the assembly lines.
  • Industrialization consultation Moving a design from prototype to repeatable production.
  • Product configuration Build-to-order configuration of finished units.
  • Materials procurement Sourcing of components and materials through ethical channels.

How Barracuda works

Owner-driven, and built on relationships.

A Close customer and supplier relationships

Barracuda is privately owned and owner-driven. Customers and suppliers deal with a company that stays close to its own work rather than one managed at arm's length.

B Agility

Assembly, inspection, rework, finishing and machining sit in one building. That keeps the loop short when a design changes or a run needs to move.

C Ethical sourcing

Components and materials are procured through ethical channels, and end-of-life electronics are handled under eWASA certification.
